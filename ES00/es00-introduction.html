What is an Embedded System?

An electronic system within a larger device

Too general

A computational system that supports the primary functions of a device

Closer, but still general. Starting to talk about the role of ES in interacting with other systems

A computational system with tight constraints on size, power consumption and functional correctness

Probably a necessary condition, but not sufficient

A computational system without keyboard or mouse

An older definition that was really trying to say "any computer that isn't a desktop or laptop". This is no longer a good line esp with mobile etc

A computational system that you don't notice until it breaks

Fun defintion, but basically talking about the use of ES in service roles. Also hints at high reliability requirements

What charactersistics do they have?

Small?

Are Boeing 787 control systems?

Also factory controls, data center monitoring etc

Fast, Low-latency?

Everything should be fast and low-latency (though the importance of those factors may change)

Multi-threaded?

Almost all software is

Efficient?

All software should be!

Low-level?

All software has, or runs on, a low level section

So therefore not sufficient definition

What charactersistics do they really have?

Heavily concurrent and/or distributed

Not just multi-threaded, but make use of this in order to match the distributed and concurrent nature of the real world

Close to the end sensors/actuators

Close enough that they have to understand how they work

Part of larger systems

Hence "embedded"

Failure often catastrophic and may lead to loss of life, environmental damage, large-scale damage to the system etc.
